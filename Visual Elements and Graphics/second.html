The solar system comprises the Sun, eight planets, five officially recognized dwarf planets, and numerous moons, asteroids, and comets. The Sun, a G-type main-sequence star, contains 99.86% of the system's known mass, exerting a dominant gravitational influence. The eight planets—Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune—vary in composition and size. The inner planets (Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars) are rocky and terrestrial, while the outer planets (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) are gas and ice giants. Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt, home to dwarf planets like Pluto, and a vast array of small icy bodies. This intricate system also includes the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter, composed of rocky remnants from the solar system's formation.
